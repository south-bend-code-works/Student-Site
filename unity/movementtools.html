Unity Cheat Sheet - Movement Tools

Forgot stuff? It happens!

Here are some tools to help you position objects in your scene. They are at the top-left.

Move Tool

Drag the arrows that appear to move the object on the X, Y, or Z axis. Y is vertical and the other two are horizontal. Key shortcut: W



Rotate Tool

Objects can be rotated by grabbing the circles around the object. Key shortcut: E



Scaling Tool

Change the size of an object. The boxes can be dragged to increase or decrease the size on each axis. If you want to scale it uniformly, drag the center box. Key shortcut: R



Other Tips

Frame in: Double-click the name of an object or press “F” while it is selected to zoom in on that object in the Scene view.

Transform Component: You can fine-tune your object’s placement with its transform component in the inspector.



Global vs. Local

When moving or rotating an object in Unity’s scene view, you can choose whether this is done in the global (absolute) or local (relative) sense. Take a look at this toggle, located right by the movement toolbar.

It says whether or not we’re in local or global mode. Look at the movement handles in global mode.

The directions are the global directions - that is, the directions of the world. The green Y-handle is pointed straight up in relation to the entire scene. Now look at local view:

This moves the object in relation to itself. Both modes are useful for different things!
