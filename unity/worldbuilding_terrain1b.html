Your Universe

Start to think about your world. The climate adds detail and depth to a story. Adding customization can make this world your own. Start to shop on the Asset Store to find trees that fit within your story.


The Standard Assets that we worked with earlier has a standard tree you can work with. If that is something you want to work with, skip this next step.


But your world isn't standard, why should your trees be?

Here are three highly recommended tree packages that will give you stunning trees that will echo the style of your realm
World Space Trees

The Trees are mostly deciduous, but the color ranges are out of this world.

Nature Starter Kit 2

Different types of trees included, grasses, bushes, flowers. This app has a lot of selection.

Nature Pack Extended

This is a fun, while unrealistic and heavily stylized asset package. If you choose a package like this, be to sure to continue the atmosphere. Low trees matched with low poly characters and collectables.

Worldbuilding Tip:

Remember continuity! Make sure your trees fit within the world you create. If you decide to try out low poly, go all out! Does your game take place during Autumn? Research. Google for photos online of the world you want to create, then try it out!


Now let's add those trees in

The Tree tool is second on the Terrain tool bar.

Add a tree model:

Edit Trees → Add Tree

This will open a pop-up window asking for a tree prefab.


In this pre-fab, there are three options: broadleaf, conifer, and palm


Go to Assets → Standard Assets → Environment → Speedtree → Your trees

Find the tree prefab, which is the one with the icon featuring a 3D tree and a grey background.

(Or drag in your own custom tree if you aren't using the Standard Assets)

Drag that prefab into the box asking for the tree prefab in the Add a Tree menu.


Trees are painted just like the terrain. There are various new options:

Tree Density - how many trees you're placing in the painted area.

Tree Height - how tall the tree is. This can be random and the range can be dragged.

Random Tree Rotation - having random tree rotation is good for variety.


Recommendation: Trees are pretty resource intensive. Having too many can really slow your game down or even lead to a crash. Be careful.

Adding Water

Water is an amazing way to make your terrain look more realistic.

Go back into your terrain, adjust your brush.

Create an indent in the terrain where you want your water to be.

Does it make sense in your story to have just one pond? To have several? A river?

Get the water from: Standard Assets → Environment → Water → Water → Prefabs
Worldbuilding Tip:

When does your game take place? Choose here between daytime and nighttime water.


From there, drag it onto your scene and adjust its position and size with the menu

Can't remember how to move it? check out the Movement Tools Cheat Sheet!

Lighting

To edit lighting, select the Directional Light that came with your scene in the Hierarchy.

To edit lighting, select the Directional Light that came with your scene in the Hierarchy.

Rotation - Can edit this in the Inspector's transform component. This is the angle for the light. Directional light's positioning and scale do not affect their lighting.

Intensity - How bright the light is. This is on the Light component in the Inspector.

Color - The color of the light. Can be adjusted on the Light component in the Inspector.

Worldbuilding Tip:

When does your game take place? For a daytime setting, white light is good. For dawn, dusk, or night, the color could be an orange or a blue. Play around with the settings and shop on the Asset Store, dynamic lighting can be a facinating way to tell a story.


Skybox: Mold your horizons

A dynamic horizon is one of the best ways to make a player fall head over heels for your universe. Including a thoughtfully picked out skybox is also a fantastic way to worldbuild.

Jaw dropping skys are a great way to center your story, give a bit of backstory, and really make a world come together.

Skyrim put a lot of work into immersing the character within the story, and the sky was a key part
Witcher 3 and God of War are also great examples of using the scenery and their surroundings to tell a story.
Sky Shopping

Your sky

So let's go back to your notes. Start to think about your world. The climate, the story, the timeline. Is it post-apocalyptic? Do you plan on having different levels where the sky changes subtly and get more ominous? The Asset Store is a great place to find skies that fit within your story.

Here are three highly highly detailed and dynamic skies that will help you mold the story and build a backbone to your realm.

Skybox Series Free

This series has a good range of different skies. This pack has it all from night sky galaxy to mid horizon and underwater. This is a great pack if you plan on doing different levels or simply want variety.

Ultra Skybox Fog Shade

Fog is a stunning way to look everything from ominous to full of hope. Fog has a lot of potential for worldbuilding and storytelling.

Skybox Volume 2
