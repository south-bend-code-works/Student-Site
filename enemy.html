Creating a NavMesh

You may notice that our enemy still isn't following the player! This is because it doesn't have a NavMesh. You've made your own complex terrain and the enemy doesn't know where it can walk on it! A NavMesh, short for navigation mesh, will tell the enemy where it can walk.

//NavMeshes are how Unity handles pathfinding - the path an enemy chooses

To create one, go to the menu at the top of the screen.

Window → AI → Navigation

This will open it as a tab above the Inspector.

Go to the Object tab. For now, all you have to do is make sure your terrain and any other objects that impact navigation (any buildings, water, or other environmental objects) are selected. Be sure to not select any interactive items like Controllers or Collectables! To select multiple things in the Hierarchy, hold Ctrl and click each one. They will show up on the Object tab as you select them.

Make sure they are all marked as Navigation Static then go to the Bake tab and click Bake. This process will take a bit, and then you'll see that your terrain has blue areas marked. These are the areas in which your enemy can walk.

//Note: You'll have to rebake this NavMesh any time you adjust the terrain.

Now that your enemy has a target and a NavMesh, they'll start chasing your player! You can adjust their speed in the Third Person Character component.
