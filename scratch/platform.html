Platform Programming

We're going to start by programming a sprite to jump when the up arrow key is pressed and fall when it's not touching a platform.

To move the sprite in different directions, we'll use the X & Y coordinates.

Click on your sprite.

First, let's grab a change y by __ block. We want the sprite to be able to jump as high as the first platform, change the value so that our sprite can jump.

Now we want to grab another change y by __ block, that way gravity can bring our sprite back down. Be sure to change the value to a negative.
  • infoI need a hint

Next, add a when the up arrow key is pressed block, attach this to your first change y by __ block.

Now, we need to program the game to recognize the platforms. Grab an if/then block, attach this to your second change y by __ block.

Get a touching color block, fit this inside your if/then block.

But hold on, we want to get a not block, that way our sprite will drop if they are not on the block.

Don't forget to change the color! Click on the paint chip and a new menu will pop up.

Click on the eyedropper at the bottom, a new cursor will appear and hover over the platform color.

That changed the color!

Now we need to tell our program when to run this code, grab a when green flag clicked block. Don't forget to grab a forever block around our if statement.
  • infoI need a hint

Test your code!

When you press the upper arrow, does the sprite move up?

Does your sprite fall when not on a platform?

Moving our sprite left and right

Drag a change x by ____ block, this will allow for our sprite to move right. Do the same again, grab a change x by ____ block, but change the value to a negative.

That way you can move left and right!

Go into events and get a when ___ key pressed block for each direction.

Don't forget to change your blocks so that when you press the left arrow, your sprite will go left and change your other block so that when you press the right arrow, you can go right.

Test your code!

When you press the right arrow, does the sprite move to the right?

Does your sprite also move to the left?

It's a little bumpy though, so let's fix that.
Add a repeat until block around our first change x by ___ motion block.

Add in a not and key right arrow pressed in the diamond.

This will make for a smoother transition when the right arrow is pressed

Go ahead and do the same thing for the left side as well.

Creating a win

We programmed our sprite to be able to reach the top, but nothing happens when we touch our prize. Let's fix that.

Add an if ____ then block to our When green flag clicked code.

Find a touching ______ block and select our prize from the drop down.

Add a say ___ for 2 seconds block in the if ____ then block.
