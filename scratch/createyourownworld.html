Coding Your World

Let's allow the player to walk through doors into other rooms!

Your project contains backdrops for additional rooms:

You'll need a new 'for all sprites' variable called 'room', to keep track of what room the player is in.

When the player touches the orange door in the first room, the next backdrop should be displayed, and the player should move back to the left side of the stage. Here are the blocks you'll need - they should go inside the player's forever loop

Add code to the start of your player code (before the forever loop) that resets your player's position on the screen and the room that they're in.

Click the flag and move your player over the orange door. does your player move to the next screen? Does the room variable change to two?
Challenge: Moving to the Previous Room

Can you make your player move to the previous room when they touch a yellow door? Remember that this code will be very similar to the code you've already added for moving to the next room.

Save your project
Signs

Let's add signs to your world, to guide your player on their journey.

Your starter project includes a welcome sign sprite:

This sign will only be visible in room one, so let's add some code to the sign to make sure that it shows if in room one, and hides if in any other room.

Test your sign by moving between rooms. Your sign should only be visible in room one.

A sign isn't much good if it doesn't say anything! Let's add some more code (in another separate block) to display a message if the sign is touching the player.

Test out your sign, and you should see a message when the player touches it.

Save your project
Challenge: Treasure!

Right-click on the treasure chest sprite and choose 'show'.

Can you make the treasure chest sprite appear only in room three and say 'Well done!' when the player touches it?

Save your project
People

Let's add other people to your world that your player can interact with.

Add code to the person sprite, so that the person talks to your player when you touch them.

You could also allow your person to move back and forth, continuously.
Challenge: Improving Your Person

Can you add code to your new person, so that they only appear in room one? Make sure you test out your new code!

Save your project

You can also add in patrolling enemies, who end the game when you touch them. Select your enemy sprite.

Add code to your enemy, so that they only appear in room two.

You'll also need to add code to move the enemy, and to end the game if the enemy touches the player.

Test out your enemy, to make sure that:
  • It's only visible in room 2;
  • It patrols the room;
  • The game ends if the player touches it.

Save your project
Challenge: More enemies

Can you create another enemy in room three that patrols up and down through the gap in the wall?

Save your project
Collecting Coins

Add a new variable called coins to your project.

Right-click on the 'coin' sprite and choose 'show'.

Add code to your coin, so that it only appears in room one.

Add more code to your coin, that will add one to your 'coins' variable once your character collects it.

Have your coin hide once your character collects it.

You'll also need to add code to set your 'coins' variable to zero at the start of your game.

Test your project - collecting your coin should change your 'coins' score to one!
Challenge: More Coins

Can you add more coins to your game? They can be in different rooms, and some coins could even be guarded by patrolling enemies.

Save your project
Doors and Keys

Right click the key sprite and choose 'show' so that it appears on the stage. Switch your stage to backdrop three, and place the key somewhere difficult to reach!

Make sure that your key is only visible in room three.

Create a new list variable called 'inventory'. This will be where you store all of the items your player collects.

The code for collecting the key is similar to the code for collecting coins. The difference is that you add the key to your inventory.

Test out your key to see if you can collect it, and add it to your inventory.

Remember to add code to your stage to empty your inventory at the start.

Place your blue door sprite across the gap in the two walls.

Add code to your door, so that it is only visible in room three.

You'll need to hide your blue door to allow your player to pass once you have the key in your inventory.

Test out your project, and see if you can collect the key to open the door!

Save your project
Challenge: Create Your Own World!

You can continue creating your world. Here are some ideas:

Change the settings and graphics of your game;

Add sound and music;

Add more people, enemies, signs, and coins

Add color-coded doors, that need their own keys to open

Add more rooms;

Add other items to collect and use;

Spend your coins to get information or items from people;

You could even add north and south doors, so that the player could move between rooms in every direction.

Save your project
