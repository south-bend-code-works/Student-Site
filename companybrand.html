Design the Brand & Identity of a Company or Start-up

In this lesson you will:
  1. Learn about brands
  2. Create a new logo

Do you think that it'd be cool to start your company or design a logo for a friend's business?

To get started, there's a few things that you should know:

Branding is what other people think about you, your product, and your service.

Visual identity is what your brand looks like, including: logo, color choices, etc.

Your tone can be classy and refined.

Or a little more out there!

Every element (logo, images, colors, and fonts) work together to say what your brand is about.
Got those? Great!

Hierarchy

You want fonts to add to your company's branding, not take away from it. For typography to help do this, use hierarchy.

Leading

Leading is the space between lines of text. Designers use leading to help make the text easier to read.

Tracking

Tracking is the space between each letter or character.

Kerning

Kerning is the space between specific characters and caries through the course of the word.

Step #7 - Design Review + Critique

The critique is a fundamental part of the design process. This helps you discuss ideas out with your peers and instructors. Be prepared for criticism.

It's time to get feedback!

Peer Feedback

Choose two peers. Ask them for honest feedback on your logo. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Instructor Feedback

Ask one of your instructors to critique your design. Set up a time to meet and get back together to chat about your logo. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Step #8 - Revise

Every design has revisions and changes. No one gets it right the first time.

Revise your logo and make it a product that you feel happy with.
