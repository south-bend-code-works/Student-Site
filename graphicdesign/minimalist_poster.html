Minimalist Poster

In this lesson you will:
  1. Create a Minimalist Poster

A cluttered design is like a cluttered desk – you can never find what you need, or if you find it than you spend a lot of time looking for it.

Here are examples of minimalist posters:
Design Challenge
Try creating a minimalist poster, that meets the following criteria:
Your friend started a band called the Energetic 6.5s. They have their first paid gig coming up and need a poster. They want to wow the audience, and are relying on your expertise as a graphic designer. Your friend wants a poster that is minimalist, has lots of negative space, and uses one color to pop. Use your newly gained knowledge to create a 16x20in poster in GIMP.
Get Started!

Open GIMP

Open a new project.

Create a 16x20in poster.

Start designing a logo for them. It's a good idea to use their band name.

Add a concert location and time to the poster.
Design Review + Critique

The critique is a fundamental part of the design process. This helps you discuss ideas out with your peers and instructors. Be prepared for criticism.

It's time to get feedback!

Peer Feedback

Choose two peers. Ask them for honest feedback on your poster. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Instructor Feedback

Ask one of your instructors to critique your design. Set up a time to meet and get back together to chat about your poster. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Revise

Every design has revisions and changes. No one gets it right the first time.

Revise your poster and make it a product that you feel happy with.
