Geometric Artwork Poster

In this lesson you will:
  1. Learn about geometric artwork
  2. Create your own geometric poster
Step #1 - Choose Your Image

Pick a high-quality image from Canva with:

- Multiple colors or shades of colors

- A large subject. Don't choose a person standing at the edge of the frame or in the distance. Faces work really well.
Step #2 - Open Your Image in GIMP

In GIMP, in the top-left corner, select "File" then "Open".

A box will pop up. Select your photo.
Step #3 - Explore GIMP's Tools
The main tools that we will be using are the Lasso Tool and the Eye dropper tool.

Use the lasso tool to "lasso" a triangle.

Use the eyedropper tool to select the main color in the area that you just selected with the lasso.

This becomes the foreground color.

Now, fill the triangle with the color you just selected.
Step #4 - Repeat

Keep using the lasso tool to make more triangles and the eye dropper tool to fill each triangle.
Step #5 - Cut Out Your Image

Once you finish all of the triangles, cut out the image you just made by right clicking on the image's little bar, in the box on the right hand side.

Click "Add Alpha Channel".

This adds a layer of translucency to the image.

Using your lasso tool, carefully, select the outside of the entire image.

Go into the Select option in the top right hand corner, and choose Invert.
Cut out the area around your image, leaving just your subject.
Step #6 - Make Your Background

If you want your project to have a background-color, first, choose your background-color.

At the bottom of the toolbox (the thing on the left), you will see which color you last used.

Click the color UNDER it (which should be black or white) and choose the color that you want to be the background.

You'll need to add a layer in the box on the right hand side. Click that little folder.

Choose background-color under the "Layer Fill Type" section and click ok.

Click and drag the color behind your main image
Step #7 - Save

Save your project.
Step #8 - Design Review + Critique

The critique is a fundamental part of the design process. This helps you discuss ideas out with your peers and instructors. Be prepared for criticism.

It's time to get feedback!

Peer Feedback

Choose two peers. Ask them for honest feedback on your poster. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Instructor Feedback

Ask one of your instructors to critique your design. Set up a time to meet and get back together to chat about your poster. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Step #9 - Revise

Every design has revisions and changes. No one gets it right the first time.

Revise your poster and make it a product that you feel happy with.
Why is Geometric Artwork important?
Visuals that are not only unique in their subject matter, but how they are executed, captivate us and make us more interested and engaged.

Geometric vector art can be challenging, but if you can deliver something truly unique, the payoff is totally worth it.

A client may not request this type of artwork, but they are sure to be impressed by it if it’s done well.
