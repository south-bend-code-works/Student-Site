T Shirt Mockups

In this lesson you will:
  1. Create a T Shirt Mockup

Let's make some T Shirts!

Let's get started!

Open GIMP

Design a logo in GIMP or use a logo that you've previously designed.

Right click on this image and Download this picture.
The image is a PNG file with a transparent background, so it should open in GIMP with a checkerboard background.
**Make sure that the design you import is a PNG file with a transparent background.**

Import your logo as a new layer and set the blend mode to Darken Only.

This will make it appear as if the logo is printed onto the shirt, with crease lines and shadows included.

That was pretty simple, but what about changing the color of the shirt?
Create a new layer above the shirt layer, set the mode to Multiply, then fill the layer in with whatever color you’d like the shirt to be.

But wait...
Our logo has changed as well. How do we fix that?

To fix this, we just have to delete a segment of the color layer so that the logo and can show through as it’s supposed to.

Right click on the layer with the logo on it and select Alpha to Selection.
This is going to create a selection around your logo.

Click on the color layer and press Delete on your keyboard.

The logo will now show through the deleted area.

Great work! You created your own t-shirt mockup!

Review + Critique

The critique is a fundamental part of the design process. This helps you discuss ideas out with your peers and instructors. Be prepared for criticism.

It's time to get feedback!

Peer Feedback

Choose two peers. Ask them for honest feedback on your t-shirt design. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Instructor Feedback

Ask one of your instructors to critique your design. Set up a time to meet and get back together to chat about your logo. Ask them what they like. What they don't like. Take notes, and ask what they would change.
Revise

Every design has revisions and changes. No one gets it right the first time.

Revise your logo and make it a product that you feel happy with.
