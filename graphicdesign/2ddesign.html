Lines


Open GIMP

Start to experiment with lines

Use the paintbrush tool on the left panel

Play with weight, color, texture, and style (click on the link to go to the glossary for a refresher on what those terms are)


Produce three different examples of different weights, colors, textures, and styles
Takeaway: Practical Applications

This lesson might seem rather simplistic. Lines are very basic, but they are a good starting point. Out in the wild, looking at websites and apps, we see designs that work, but can't exactly pin point why. As a fresh Graphic Designer you will start to notice these concepts in your every day world. Lines, as simple as they are, are fundamental to Web Design, Infographics, and believe it or not, but Text & Typography

Shapes

Open GIMP

Find 6 items around you, 3 geometric and 3 organic.

Search google or look around you, and start to look at them as abstract shapes (Abstract shapes are the large, basic shapes that provide the building blocks for virtually any painting or design).

Not sure how to add shapes?

Takeaway: Practical Applications

Shapes, again another very simplistic tool in the toolbelt of the skilled Graphic Designer. Many logos and graphical elements used in websites and printed materials are abstract shapes. Abstraction is a hard concept to wrap your fresh Graphic Designer head around, but in time, you will be able to break up common, everyday items into pleasing shapes and clean designs.

Rule of Thirds

Many people, including designers and photographers, use a strategy called the rule of thirds. This imagines your work area divided into a 3x3 grid

Takeaway: Practical Applications

The Rule of Thirds is a common design strategy that simply looks clean, organized, and gets the subject matter across to the viewer efficently. Balance is a key part of Graphic Design, and as a fresh Graphic Designer, using the Rule of Thirds to balance a project out is a great stepping stone until you start getting your feet wet with other tools of the trade.
