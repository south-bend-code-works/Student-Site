Create your own GIF

In this lesson you will:
  1. Create your very own gif

Very few things grab our attention as much as moving objects.

GIFs are fun, eye-catching, and can be informative.

When you break down a GIF, it's nothing more than a sequence of images with a delay inbetween.

This lesson will allow you to bring your graphics to life with GIMP and make a simple GIF.

Step #1 - Plan

Find images that you can use for creating the animated GIF file.

Save the images to your Code School folder.

Rename the images with the number corresponding to the place it is going to have in your animation.

For example, the first picture in your GIF should be named one, the second one should be named two, the third one should be named three and so on.
Step #2 - Build

Open GIMP.

Open the images that you want to use for the animated GIF in GIMP.

To do that, open the File menu and click or tap on Open as Layers.

When all the pictures are added, to get a preview of how your animated GIF file is going to look, open the Filters menu, go to Animation, and click or tap Playback.

In the newly opened window, click or tap Play to preview your animated GIF file.
To end the preview, close the Animation Playback window.

Save your animated GIF file.

Open the File menu again, and click or tap Export Asâ€¦

In the Export Image window:

- Select the location where you want to save your GIF file
- Name your file
- Specify the GIF extension at the end (for example: animation.gif)

Then, click or tap Export.

GIMP now opens a new dialog, called "Export Image as GIF".

Make sure you check the option called "As animation".

Also, set the delay used between the frames of your animation. When you have chosen all the settings that you want, click or tap Export.

Depending on how many pictures you used and their sizes, you might have to wait for a couple of moments for GIMP to save your animated GIF. The GIF file is now saved and ready to use!
