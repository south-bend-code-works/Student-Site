What makes a bad font, bad?

You've been using a lot of different typefaces in your designs, and we have been telling you what's good, what's bad along the way. Though, how are you able to tell a good font from a bad one?

One word: legiblity

Part of what makes a strong font is it's readability. There are other factors, like how fresh or new the font seems, or if it's been overused.

If it's easier to read, people will straight up want to.


But how do you pick a winning font?

Think about a few simple things...

Overused: When a typeface is overused, it gets tired. Massive miss-use or over-use makes it ordinary and annoying.

Kerning: That space between letters can add up. While you can fix the kerning manually, it is time consuming and is a hallmark of a bad typeface.

Legibility: If it's illegible, people have to make a huge effort to read what you're trying to say. It's easy to lose interest along the way, even if it's fancy. Cursive is a great example.
